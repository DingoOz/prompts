# NotebookLM Accelerated Learning Method

A structured prompt sequence for compressing deep subject mastery into hours instead of months. Upload as many sources as possible — textbooks, papers, lecture transcripts — then work through these phases in order.

*Source: viral X post about an MIT grad student's NotebookLM workflow*

---

## Phase 1: Map the Expert Mental Models

Start here. Don't ask for summaries — ask for the thinking frameworks that experts internalize over years.

```
What are the 5 core mental models that every expert in this field shares?
```

---

## Phase 2: Map the Disagreements

This gives you the intellectual landscape — the debates, consensus, and open questions that most students spend an entire semester just identifying.

```
Show me the 3 places where experts in this field fundamentally disagree, and what each side's strongest argument is.
```

---

## Phase 3: Generate Deep Understanding Questions

These separate real comprehension from surface-level memorization. Use them as your study guide.

```
Generate 10 questions that would expose whether someone deeply understands this subject versus someone who just memorized facts.
```

---

## Phase 4: Iterative Self-Testing

Work through each question from Phase 3 using the source material. When you get something wrong, use this follow-up to close the gap:

```
Explain why this is wrong and what I'm missing.
```

Repeat until you can answer every question confidently.

---

## The Key Insight

The tool doesn't change. The questions do. Most people use NotebookLM like a fancy highlighter. This workflow uses it like a private tutor who has read everything ever written on the subject. The difference between a semester and 48 hours isn't the amount of content — it's knowing which questions to ask.
